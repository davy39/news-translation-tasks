Sometimes you might want to compose an email but send it later. Well, did you know that Gmail allows you to schedule up to 100 emails?

In this article, you will learn how to schedule your emails using Gmail. There's no need to install any software or plugins.

## What you will learn

* [How to schedule an email in Gmail](#heading-how-to-schedule-an-email-in-gmail)
    
* [How to view or edit a scheduled email in Gmail](#how-to-view-or-edit-a-scheduled-email-in-gmail)
    
* [How to cancel a scheduled email in Gmail](#heading-how-to-cancel-a-scheduled-email-in-gmail)
    

## How to Schedule an Email in Gmail

**Step 1:** Log in to your Gmail account.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/1.-login.png align="left")

**Step 2:** Click compose to create your email.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/2.-click-compose.png align="left")

**Step 3:** Create the email you want to schedule.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/3.-create-email.PNG align="left")

**Step 4:** Click the Down arrow on the `Send` button.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/4.-click-arrow.png align="left")

**Step 5:** Click `Schedule send`.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/5.-schedule-send.png align="left")

**Step 6:** The popup modal give you a few options. You can choose one. Or you can create a custom date/time by clicking `Select date and time`.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/6.-select-date-time.png align="left")

**Step 7:** Click the `Schedule send` button.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/7.-schedule-send2.png align="left")

That's it! Your email is now scheduled. You will see a notification from Gmail (like the one below) confirming a successful schedule.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/8.-success-schedule.PNG align="left")

Next, let's see how you can edit a scheduled mail in Gmail. This can be either editing the content of the mail or changing the scheduled time.

## How to Edit a Scheduled Email in Gmail

Assuming you are already logged in to your Gmail, continue with the steps below. If not, first login and then continue with the steps below.

**Step 1:** To edit a scheduled email, click `Scheduled` on the left panel.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/scheduled.png align="left")

**Step 2:** You will be presented with a list of your scheduled emails. Select the email you want to edit.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/schedule-list.PNG align="left")

**Step 3:** Click `Cancel send` at the top right of your email.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/cancel-send.png align="left")

**Step 4:** Make the changes if you want to edit the content of your email. If you only want to change the scheduled time, move to Step 5.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/edit-schedule.PNG align="left")

**Step 5:** Next, you use the same process you used for the initial schedule.

* Click the down arrow next to the `Send` button.
    
* And then click `Schedule send`.
    
* Choose a new date and time.
    

That's it! You've successfully edited the scheduled email.

## How to Cancel a Scheduled Email in Gmail

Assuming you are already logged in to your Gmail, continue with the steps below. If not, first login and then continue with the steps below.

**Step 1:** To cancel a scheduled email, click `Scheduled` on the left panel.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/scheduled-1.png align="left")

**Step 2:** You will be presented with a list of your scheduled emails. Select the email you want to cancel.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/schedule-list-1.PNG align="left")

**Step 3:** Click `Cancel send` at the top right of your email.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/cancel-send-1.png align="left")

Note that cancelling a scheduled email in Gmail does not delete it. Gmail treats it as a draft and adds it to the drafts folder. See the Gmail notification below.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/11/msg-revert.PNG align="left")
