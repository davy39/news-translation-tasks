So, you’re working with your Windows PC and you discover you can’t find the battery icon in the taskbar.
![ss1](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss1.png)

You even expand hidden icons and you still can’t find the battery icon.
![ss2](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss2.png)

This could result in a negative effect on your productivity – because if you don't plug your laptop in, the battery might run down and you wouldn’t know.

I just had the same issue and fixed it. So, in this article, I’ll show you how to restore a missing battery icon on a Windows PC.

## Solution 1: Show the Battery Icon through Taskbar Settings

**Step 1**: Right-click on an empty space in the taskbar and select “Taskbar Settings”.
![ss3](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss3.png)

**Step 2**: Under “Notification area”, click on “Turn system icons on or off”.
 ![ss4](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss4.png)

**Step 3**: Toggle on "Power".
![toggle-on-battery-icon](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/toggle-on-battery-icon.gif)

**NB**: If the battery icon is greyed out here, proceed to the next fix in this article.
![ss9](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss9.png)

## Solution 2: Disable and Re-enable Battery Hardware

**Step 1**: Right-click on Start and select Device Manager.
![ss5](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss5.png)
 
**Step 2**: Expand Batteries, right-click on “Microsoft AC Adapter”, and select “Disable device”.
![ss6](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss6.png)

You will see a warning. Click Yes to continue.
![ss7](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss7.png)
 
**Step 3**: Repeat the process in **step 2** for the second device – Microsoft ACPI Compliant Control Method Battery.

**Step 4**: Re-enable both devices.
![ss8](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/ss8.png)

**Step 5**: Restart your computer.


## Conclusion

If both fixes suggested in this article failed to restore the battery icon, you should update your Windows.
