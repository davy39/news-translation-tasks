By Dhawal Shah

If you haven’t heard, universities around the world are offering their courses online for free (or at least, partially free). These [online courses](https://www.classcentral.com/help/intro-to-online-learning) are collectively called MOOCs or [Massive Open Online Courses.](https://www.classcentral.com/help/moocs)

In the past ten years or so, over 1000 universities have created more than 20,000 such online courses. I’ve been keeping track of them this entire time here at [Class Central](https://www.classcentral.com/), a search engine and reviews site for online education which has been used by [60 million learners](https://www.classcentral.com/about) around the world.

In the past six months alone, more than 200 universities — including Harvard, Stanford, MIT, and a few Ivy League Schools — have announced over 1000 such free online courses. I’ve compiled this list below and categorized the courses into the following subjects:

* [Computer Science](#heading-computer-science-81) (84)
* [Programming](#heading-programming-54) (54)

* [Personal Development](#heading-personal-development-38) (38)
* [Humanities](#heading-humanities-87) (87)
* [Social Sciences](#heading-social-sciences-89) (89)
* [Health & Medicine](#heading-health-amp-medicine-91) (91)
* [Education & Teaching](#heading-education-amp-teaching-21) (21)
* [Data Science](#heading-data-science-39) (39)
* [Mathematics](#heading-mathematics-32) (32)
* [Art & Design](#heading-art-amp-design-27) (27)
* [International](#heading-international-130) (130)
