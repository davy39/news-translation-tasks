Enabling and disabling the search highlights on Windows 10 is pretty straightforward. But the same thing can't be said for Windows 11.

## How to Disable Search Highlights on Windows 11

Firstly, click on the start button where your windows logo is ðŸªŸ.   
Then click on the search box once.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/Screenshot-2022-07-15-181846.png)

You will get the options menu just right beside your user icon. Click there.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/Screenshot-2022-07-15-182242.png)

Click `Search settings`. The search permissions tab will appear.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/Screenshot-2022-07-15-182356.png)

Scroll down until you find the `Show search highlights` section.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/Screenshot-2022-07-15-182443.png)

Simply disable that. And that's it!

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/Screenshot-2022-07-15-182532.png)

The search highlights will not appear again.

## How to Enable Search Highlights on Windows 11

Open the `Search Permission` section like earlier. Then scroll down until you find the `Show search highlights` section. Simply enable that! As simple as that.

![Image](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/content/images/2022/07/Screenshot-2022-07-15-182735.png)
