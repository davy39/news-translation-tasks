## What is a Hacker?

![Hats on silhoettes](https://miro.medium.com/max/1400/1*Wme1T4nYK463bfqHVjAHvA.jpeg)
_Hats on Silhoettes | Credit: Wallpaperflare.com_

A hacker is an individual who uses their skills to breach cybersecurity defences. In the world of Cybersecurity, hackers are typically classified by a ‚Äòhat‚Äô system. This system likely came from old cowboy film culture where the good characters typically wore white hats and the bad ones wore black hats.

There are 3 major hats in the cyberspace:

1. White Hats 
2. Grey Hats
3. Black Hats

However, there are some others that have also cropped up over time such as:

1. Green Hats
2. Blue Hats

## White Hat Hackers

![Captain America](https://miro.medium.com/max/1400/1*Q_3O7-T-Ka3H3VEigqmSPQ.jpeg)
_Captain America | Credit: Wallpaperaccess.com_

White hats are just like Marvel‚Äôs Captain America üõ°Ô∏è. No matter the day, time, or age, they always stand up for what‚Äôs right and protect civilians and organizations at large by finding and reporting vulnerabilities in systems before the black hats do. 

They usually work for organizations and take roles such as a Cybersecurity Engineer, Penetration Tester, Security Analyst, CISO (Chief Information Security Officer), and other security positions.

Under these organizations they perform tasks such as:

1. Scanning networks
2. Configuring IDSs (Intrusion Detection Systems)
3. Ethically hacking computers to find vulnerabilities and report them so they can be addressed
4. Programming honeypots (Traps for the attackers üòº)
5. Monitoring network activity for suspicious activity

Famous examples of such hackers include:

1. [Jeff Moss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Moss_(hacker)) (DEF CON founder)
2. [Richard Stallman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Stallman) (Founder of the GNU project)
3. [Tim Burners-Lee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tim_Berners-Lee) (Creator of the World Wide Web)
4. [Linus Torvalds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linus_Torvalds) (Creator of Linux)
5. [Tsutomu Shimomura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsutomu_Shimomura) (The man that caught Kevin Mitnick)

And if you want to hear more from the founder of a cybersecurity company herself, [check out this podcast featuring Rachel Tobac](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/podcast-rachel-tobac/).

## Grey Hat Hackers

![Image](https://miro.medium.com/max/1400/1*VPb8xXtNyyOIswm_Syvi8w.jpeg)
_Batman | Credit: Alphacoders.com_

DCs‚Äô Dark Knight and grey hat hackers have a lot in common ü¶á. They both want to stand up for the right thing but use rather unconventional methods to do so. 

Grey hat hackers are the balance between white hats and black hats. In contrast to white hats, they do not ask for permission to hack systems but do not perform any other illegal activities like black hat hackers.

Grey hats have quite a controversial history. This makes them hard to really classify, especially if their moral compass goes a little haywire down the line or what they did seems more black hat-ish than white hat-ish. Some even end up in jail for what they do. 

But there are some that rise to be the heroes of the people and the enemy of the government and big organizations.

Some (in)famous examples of grey hat hackers are:

1. [Anonymous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anonymous_(hacker_group)#2022) (World famous hacktivist group)
2. [HD Moore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H._D._Moore) (Creator of Metasploit)
3. [Adrian Lamo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adrian_Lamo) (aka the homeless hacker)
4. [Khalil Shreateh](https://edition.cnn.com/2013/08/19/tech/social-media/zuckerberg-facebook-hack/index.html) (Hacked the facebook account of Mark Zuckerburg ü§£)

## Black Hat Hackers

![The Joker](https://miro.medium.com/max/1400/1*9nsMsIU_0gRjOkiwa3TxQg.jpeg)
_The Joker | Credit: Wallpapersden.com_

Time to introduce the harmful lot üÉè. The Joker and Black Hats are like peas in a pod. They perform illegal activities for financial gain, the challenge, or simply for the fun of it. 

They look for computers that are vulnerable over the internet, exploit them, and use them to whatever advantage they can.

Black Hats use techniques for getting into systems just like white hats. However, they don‚Äôt use their defensive skills ‚Äì rather, they up their game on the attack by doing things such as:

1. Installing backdoors
2. Maintaining access to compromised systems
3. Performing privilege escalation
4. Downloading private/sensitive/intellectual data
5. Installing malware such as ransomware
6. Creating phishing emails and links

Examples of infamous black hats include:

1. [Kevin Mitnick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kevin_Mitnick) (Most wanted cybercriminal in U.S history)
2. [Julian Assange](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Assange) aka Mendax (Creator of Wikileaks)
3. [Hamza Bendelladj aka Bx1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamza_Bendelladj) (Latter owner of the ZeuS Banking Malware)
4. [Kevin Poulsen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kevin_Poulsen) (Dark Dante)
5. [Robert Tappan Morris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Tappan_Morris) (Creator of the morris worm)

Mitnick, Poulsen, and Morris were criminally charged, served their sentences, and are good guys now. Mitnick founded a cybersecurity company. Poulsen created SecureDrop. And Morris became a professor at MIT (Don‚Äôt you just love a happy ending? ü§ß).

## Green Hat Hackers

![Ms Marvel](https://miro.medium.com/max/1400/1*quuAX42p8KI_4ykALHwZQA.jpeg)
_Ms Marvel | Credit: Wallpapercave.com_

Ms Marvel and Green hats are a match made in heaven üåü. They are both young, enthusiastic, inexperienced and have the tendency to take risks and learn from their mistakes. Green hats are hackers that are new to the industry but are willing to learn to become great hackers.

Because of the availability and easy of use of hacking tools these days, it's pretty easy for a green hat to end up in trouble as they may not fully understand the full workings of the tool or target. But, they learn from their errors to gather experience. 

Green hats may upgrade to White, Grey, or Black Hat hackers as they continue to move up the ranks.

## Red Hat Hackers

![Image](https://miro.medium.com/max/1400/1*kEAW09PzbhQNC0Se6EBUaA.jpeg)
_The Punisher | Credit: Wallpaperflare.com_

I think the character says it all ‚ò†. The Punisher is a ruthless anti-hero that stands up for what is right but is never ever (and I mean ever üò¨) going to give criminals second chances. 

Red hats are the same. They target cybercriminals and damage whatever they can to disable criminal activities, permanently.

Red hats are hackers no one wants to mess with, not even a black hat. Other hackers usually attack Microsoft Windows computers but these hackers, they hack Linux computers. 

They have no regrets, don‚Äôt think twice, and make black hats pay rather severely for their crimes by taking justice into their hands. They do this by destroying all data and backups of their target, and usually render the system useless.
